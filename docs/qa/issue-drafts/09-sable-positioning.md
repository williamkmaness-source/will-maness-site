## Observation

`content/projects/project-stellar.mdx` ("Sable," a 2D puzzle-action
side-scroller) is tagged `game design`, `writing`, `art direction` — no
`engineering` tag — and its summary is pure narrative pitch with no technical
framing. It's well-written, and it's not `featured` (so it only appears on
`/work`, not the homepage's Selected Work strip, which limits its exposure to
someone quickly scanning the home page).

The site's stated purpose (per `CLAUDE.md`) is demonstrating technical
fluency to VP-of-Product hiring managers at AI-infrastructure and
data-tooling companies. Sable is the one entry in the `/work` grid that
doesn't reinforce that narrative — it reads as a different kind of project
(creative/narrative) dropped into a grid otherwise organized around
engineering and data work.

This isn't a bug — it's a legitimate editorial choice (range of interests can
be a feature, not a bug, in a personal site) — flagging for a deliberate call
rather than fixing unprompted.

## Options to consider

1. Leave as-is — intentional range/personality signal.
2. Add a sentence connecting it to the technical work (tooling built for it,
   systems/design thinking, production pipeline, etc.) so it reads as
   in-narrative rather than adjacent.
3. Keep it off `/work` entirely and mention it in the About page's "currently"
   or bio copy instead, if the goal is 100% technical signal on the work grid.
